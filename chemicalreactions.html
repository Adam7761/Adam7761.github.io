Chemical Reactions

Chemical reaction is the process that occurs when two or more molecules interact, and the result is that the molecules change. What takes place is that the bonds between the atoms are broken and created to form new molecules.

Combustion Reactions

Combustion is chemical reaction that is caused by burning. Combustion reaction is an exothermic reaction, that releases energy to the surroundings. The energy released is mostly thermal energy, but light energy and sound energy are also released. There are two types of Combustion a complete combustion and an incomplete combustion: Incomplete combustion occurs when there is not enough air or oxygen for complete combustion, incomplete combustion happens instead.

Thermal Decomposition

Thermal decomposition occurs when compounds break down when they are heated, forming two or more products from one reactant. Thermal decomposition is an example of an endothermic reaction, which is a reaction that gains energy from the surroundings.

Oxidation and Reduction

Oxidation is the loss of electrons during a reaction by a molecule, atom, or ion. Oxidation occurs when the oxidation state of a molecule, atom or ion is increased. The opposite process is called reduction, which occurs when there is a gain of electrons or the oxidation state of an atom, molecule, or ion decrease.

Reactions and Catalysts

A catalyst is a substance that speeds up reactions and is not used up during the reaction its mass stays the same throughout the whole reaction and is chemically unchanged along with the mass of the catalyst after the reaction has finished. Only a very small amount of catalyst is needed to increase the rate of the reaction between large amounts of reactants. Different catalysts are required to catalyse different reactions however not all reactions have catalysts.
