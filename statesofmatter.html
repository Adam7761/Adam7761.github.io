Plasma

Plasma is very similar to gas which is the easiest way to describe plasma is that it is a gas that can carry an electrical charge. Plasma is a form of matter that exists when atoms are in an excited state. They are so excited that they jump an energy level and, in doing so, give off light. Plasma particles are spread out and move around randomly, but unlike gas, they contain some free ions and electrons, which gives plasma its ability to conduct electricity. A very well know form of plasma is lighting.

Liquid

Liquid are fluids that conform to the shape of the container that it is housed in but that retains a nearly constant volume independent of pressure. The volume does not change if the temperature and pressure are constant.

Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC)

Bose-Einstein condensate is a group of atoms cooled to within a hair of absolute zero. When they reach that temperature the atoms are hardly moving relative to each other; they have almost no free energy to do so. At that point, the atoms begin to clump together, and enter the same energy states. They become identical, from a physical point of view, and the whole group starts behaving as though it were a single atom.
